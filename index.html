Shomer Email Guardian — Privacy Policy

Last updated: [DATE] · Contact: shomer.support@gmail.com

⚠️ This draft describes how the current version of the Shomer extension actually works. Review it with a lawyer before you rely on it. It is written in plain language and is not legal advice.

Shomer Email Guardian ("Shomer", "we", "the extension") is a browser extension that helps you detect phishing emails in Gmail. This policy explains exactly what data Shomer processes, what leaves your device, and what never does.

The short version: The content of your emails is read and analyzed entirely on your own device and is never sent to us or to anyone else. To check for known threats, Shomer sends only a few technical identifiers — the links in an email, the sender's domain, and a file's fingerprint (hash) — to threat-reputation services. Your email text, your attachments, and your inbox are never uploaded.

1. What Shomer processes, and where

Scan history, sender statistics, settingsProduct functionalityOn your device only, in the browser's extension storage. Never uploaded.

2. How email analysis works

Shomer analyzes the email you are viewing in two layers, both of which run on your device:

  • Built-in detection engine: a set of on-device checks (heuristics and pattern rules) that always run locally. No email content leaves your browser for this.
  • Optional on-device AI (Gemini Nano): on supported hardware, Chrome's built-in AI model can add smart questions and a short summary. It runs locally inside your browser — email content processed by it does not leave your device. It is optional, off unless enabled, and Shomer works fully without it.

Shomer does not use any cloud AI service. Your email content is never sent to an AI provider or to any analysis server.

3. When Shomer contacts a server, and with what

The only external service Shomer's extension contacts is its own backend at shomer-backend.onrender.com, and only to relay the limited identifiers below to public threat databases. This happens only when the relevant feature is enabled:

SentTo check againstFeature
Link URL (query-stripped)Google Safe Browsing, PhishTankLink reputation
Sender domainDomain-reputation lookupSender checks
File hash (SHA-256) + extensionMalwareBazaar (abuse.ch)Attachment scanning

You can turn off link checking and attachment scanning at any time in the extension's settings. When they are off, these identifiers are not sent.

4. What we never do

  • We never send your email content — subject, body, or attachments — to us or to anyone else.
  • We never sell or share your data with advertisers.
  • We never store your emails, files, or browsing history on our servers.
  • We never read your mailbox in the background. Shomer only analyzes the email you open (and, if you enable auto-analyze, it runs automatically on the email you open — still only that email).
  • We never use any data for advertising or to train AI models.

5. Third-party services

When you use link or attachment checking, the identifiers in §3 are checked against these public threat databases via our backend. Their handling of that data is governed by their own policies:

  • Google Safe Browsing — receives a query-stripped link.
  • PhishTank — receives a query-stripped link.
  • MalwareBazaar (abuse.ch) — receives a file hash only.

6. Limited Use commitment

Shomer's use of any user data is limited to providing and improving its single purpose — detecting phishing in the email you are viewing. Specifically, Shomer:

  • Only collects and transmits the minimum technical identifiers (stripped links, sender domain, file hashes) needed for the security features you enable, each of which is clearly described in the extension's interface.
  • Never transfers your data to third parties except the threat-reputation services named in §5, and only for the security check itself.
  • Never uses or transfers your data for advertising, marketing, or any purpose unrelated to phishing detection.
  • Never sells your data.
  • Never uses your data — or allows others to use it — to train, build, or improve any generalized AI or machine-learning model.
  • Never allows humans to read your email content; analysis is automated and runs on your device.

7. Data retention

  • On our backend: reputation lookups (links, domains, hashes) are used only to return a result and are not stored as user profiles. A short-lived cache may hold a lookup result briefly to avoid repeat queries, then it is discarded.
  • On your device: scan history, sender statistics, and settings stay in your browser under your control. "Clear history" wipes them at any time.

8. Your choices

  • Turn off link checking and/or attachment scanning to stop any identifiers from being sent.
  • Turn off auto-analyze so Shomer runs only when you click to scan.
  • Use the on-device AI (where supported) or leave it off — either way, email content stays on your device.
  • Remove the extension — Chrome deletes all of Shomer's local data.

9. Children

Shomer is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13 (per COPPA), and where GDPR applies, the service is intended for users aged 16 and older. If you believe a child has used the service, contact us and we will delete any associated data.

10. Changes

We will update this page and the "Last updated" date when this policy changes. If our data-handling practices change in a way that affects you, we will disclose it in the extension before the change takes effect.
