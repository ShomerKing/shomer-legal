Shomer Email Guardian — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026 Contact: shomer.support@gmail.com

Shomer Email Guardian ("Shomer", "we") is a browser extension that helps you detect phishing emails in Gmail. This policy explains exactly what data Shomer processes, where it goes, and what never leaves your device.

1. What Shomer processes and why

Data Purpose Where it is processed
Email content you view (subject, sender, body text, links) Phishing analysis Locally first; sent to our analysis server only when cloud analysis is used (see §3)
Email authentication headers (SPF/DKIM results) Sender verification Locally, via the optional companion app. Never sent to AI providers
Links found in emails Reputation checks Sent to reputation services with query parameters removed (tokens/session IDs stripped on your device)
Files you drag into Manual Scan Malware scanning Hash checked against public databases; file content scanned on our server and not stored
Scan history, sender statistics, settings Product functionality Locally only — in your browser's extension storage. Never uploaded

2. What is masked on your device before anything is sent

Before any email content leaves your browser, Shomer masks on your device: - Credit/debit card numbers → [CARD] - National ID numbers → [ID] - Phone numbers → [PHONE] - Bank account numbers (IBAN) → [IBAN]

Link query parameters (which may contain access tokens or session identifiers) are also removed on your device.

3. AI analysis

  • On-device AI (when available): On supported hardware, analysis runs entirely inside Chrome using the browser's built-in AI model. In this mode, email content never leaves your device.
  • Cloud AI: Otherwise, masked email content (subject, sender, masked body, stripped links) is sent over HTTPS to our analysis server, which forwards it to an AI provider — Google (Gemini API) or Groq — solely to produce a risk assessment. We use API tiers under which providers do not use your content to train their models.
  • We do not store email content on our server. A short-lived cache (15 minutes) is kept in memory to avoid re-analyzing the same email, then discarded.

4. Gmail access (optional companion app)

If you install the optional companion app and connect your Google account, Shomer requests the gmail.metadata scope — access to message headers only (e.g., SPF/DKIM authentication results). Shomer cannot read message bodies or attachments through this permission.

Shomer's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically: header data is used only to display sender-authentication results to you; it is never sold, never used for advertising, and never used to train AI models.

Your Google access token is encrypted with Windows DPAPI and stored only on your computer. It is never transmitted to our servers.

6. What we never do

  • We never sell or share your data with advertisers.
  • We never store your emails, files, or browsing history on our servers.
  • We never read your mailbox in the background beyond what is needed to analyze the email you are viewing (or, if you enable Auto-Scan, the newest inbox message headers).
  • We never send unmasked personal identifiers (cards, IDs, phones) to AI providers.

7. Anonymous error reports

To detect breakages quickly (for example, when Gmail changes its page structure), Shomer sends anonymous error reports to our server: the technical error type, the code location, the extension version and browser version. Reports are sanitized on your device and on our server — email addresses, URLs and long numbers are removed before storage. No email content is ever included. You can disable error reporting in Settings at any time.

8. Data retention

  • Analysis cache: up to 15 minutes, in server memory only.
  • Local history/settings: on your device, under your control — "Clear history" wipes scan history, sender statistics and cached results at any time.
  • Companion app temp files: restricted to your Windows user account and auto-deleted within 6 hours.

9. Your choices

  • Use on-device AI only (when hardware supports it) — no email content leaves your browser.
  • Disable Auto-Scan at any time.
  • Remove the extension — all local data is deleted by Chrome; revoke Google access at https://myaccount.google.com/permissions.

10. Children

Shomer is not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13 (per COPPA), and where GDPR applies, the service is intended for users aged 16 and older. If you believe a child has used the service, contact us and we will delete any associated data.

11. Changes

We will update this page and the "Last updated" date when this policy changes. Material changes will be announced in the extension.
